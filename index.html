Hello!

I'm a fourth-year PhD student at Stanford in the Natural Language Processing group, advised by Dan Jurafsky. My main research focuses on exploring the linguistic basis of computational language methods through looking at their behaviour in multilingual settings. I'm specifically very interested in using typology to analyze the abilities of neural models cross-lingually.

I'm also working on the semantics of the modal έχω να in Modern Greek, and would love to talk to any native speakers or not about it!

I am funded by an NSF GRFP fellowship, and a Stanford Graduate Fellowship.

I did my undergraduate at Berkeley, where I got BAs in Computer Science and in History. My history thesis was based on research at the organisational archives of the League For Democracy in Greece, a London-based solidarity organisation supporting the left in the Greek Civil War. It received the Kirk Underhill Prize.
